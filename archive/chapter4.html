Chapter 4

فِعْل مَاضِي (Past Verbs)

  • • Identify who performed the action (I did, You did, They did).
  • • These "Subject" endings attach to the end of Past Tense (Madi) verbs.
  • • The u / a / i hints from Chapter 9 (مَرْفُوع, مَنْصُوب, مَجْرُور) help you hear the last syllable on verbs too.
  • • Here you learn who acted by the suffix letters on the مَاضِي stem; present-tense prefixes and suffixes are in Chapter 5.

Chapter 4 Learning Instructions
📋 Prerequisites & Guidelines
  • Eligibility: The student should already know how to read Arabic.
  • Methodology: This must be done with hand-pointing actions. All students repeat together loudly.
  • Objective: To create an automated muscle memory for the 8 Arabic pronouns in past tense verbs (excluding feminine forms for now).
Important Note

Feminine forms should be strictly avoided at this stage. They will be taught in later chapters to avoid cognitive overload.

🔄 1. Base Verb Practice Routine (فَعَلَ)
1
Verb & Action Only (10x)

Repeat the past verbs of فَعَلَ for all 8 pronouns 10 times with hand actions and memorize.

2
Arabic + Action + Uttering Meaning (5x)

Instructor explains the meaning. Everyone repeats the Arabic verb and the meaning with actions 5 times.

3
Arabic + Action + Thinking Meaning (10x)

Repeat the Arabic verb and action while thinking of the meaning silently (without uttering it) 10 times.

🔄 2. Practice with 5 Additional Verbs

Apply the routine to the following 5 sound verbs:

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
كَتَبَ kataba He wrote
جَلَسَ jalasa He sat
ضَرَبَ daraba He hit / struck
شَرِبَ shariba He drank
دَرَسَ darasa He studied / learned

For each verb, follow this sequence:

A
10x with hand action.
B
5x with hand action and uttering the meaning.
C
10x with hand action and thinking the meaning (silent).
