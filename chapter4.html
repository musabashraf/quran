Feminine Pattern Tips
1. Attached Suffixes

Attached feminine pronouns (Col 2) like هَا (her) or كِ (your) are added to the end of nouns or particles.

2. Past Tense Ends

The Past tense (Col 3) feminine forms use distinctive suffixes: تْ (she), تَا (they two), نَ (they all), تِ (you), and تُنَّ (you all).

3. Present Tense Starts & Ends

The Present tense (Col 4) feminine forms include prefixes like تَـ and endings like ـينَ or ـْنَ (e.g., تَفْعَلِينَ, يَفْعَلْنَ).

The Root Formula

Just like masculine forms, feminine forms follow the 3-letter root formula. Here, we use ف-ع-ل. Simply swap these three letters with any other root (like خ-ل-ق) and the suffixes/prefixes remain exactly the same!

Chapter 4 Learning Instructions
📋 Prerequisites & Guidelines
  • Eligibility: The student should already know how to read Arabic.
  • Methodology: This must be done with hand-pointing actions. All students repeat together loudly.
  • Objective: To create an automated muscle memory for feminine pronouns and verb tense conjugations.
Important Note

First-person pronouns and verb conjugations are gender-neutral. They were covered in Chapter 3 and are omitted from this chapter to focus exclusively on feminine-specific structures.

🔄 1. Base Verb Practice Routine (فَعَلَتْ / تَفْعَلُ)
1
Verbs & Action Only (10x)

Repeat the past and present verbs together of فَعَلَ for all 6 feminine pronouns 10 times with hand actions and memorize.

2
Arabic + Action + Uttering Meaning (5x)

Instructor explains the meaning. Everyone repeats the Arabic verbs and their meanings with actions 5 times.

3
Arabic + Action + Thinking Meaning (10x)

Repeat the Arabic verbs and actions while thinking of the meanings silently (without uttering them) 10 times.

🔄 2. Practice with 5 Additional Verbs

Apply the routine to the following 5 sound verbs (both Past and Present feminine forms):

Arabic (Past / Present Fem.) Transliteration Meaning
كَتَبَتْ / تَكْتُبُ katabat / taktubu She wrote / writes
جَلَسَتْ / تَجْلِسُ jalasat / tajlisu She sat / sits
ضَرَبَتْ / تَضْرِبُ darabat / tadribu She hit / hits
