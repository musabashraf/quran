Chapter 4

Attached Pronouns (ضَمَائِر مُتَّصِلَة)

  • • Identify whose it is or who is being acted upon (Him, Us, You, Me).
  • • Appear only in the end of nouns, verbs, or particles (suffix).
  • • Master these "connectors" using the simple HUNAKANEE rule.

الَّذِي خَلَقَكُمْ وَالَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِكُمْ (2:21)

The One who created you and those before you

നിങ്ങളെയും നിങ്ങളുടെ മുൻഗാമികളെയും സൃഷ്ടിച്ചവൻ

وَلَا تُبْطِلُوا أَعْمَالَكُمْ (47:33)

And do not invalidate your deeds

നിങ്ങളുടെ കർമ്മങ്ങളെ നിങ്ങൾ പാഴാക്കരുത്

Types of Pronouns (ضَمَائِر)
Attached Pronoun (ضَمِير مُتَّصِل)

These function as connectors that attach to the end of words to show ownership or objects.

Function Ownership / Object
Example رَبُّنَا (Our Lord)
Independent Pronoun (ضَمِير مُنْفَصِل)

These are separate words that stand alone, usually as the subject of a sentence.

Function Subject (I, He, You)
Example أَنَا (I am)
